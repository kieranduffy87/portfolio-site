Web Design

Engineers Ireland

A comprehensive digital transformation for Ireland's professional engineering body, creating a modern, accessible digital platform that serves as the definitive hub for Ireland's engineering community.

Client
Engineers Ireland
Services
Web Design, UX Strategy, Development, Content Architecture
Year
2024
Location
Dublin, Ireland

The Challenge

Engineers Ireland represents over 100,000 professional engineers across the country, serving as the primary body advancing the engineering profession through membership, professional development, advocacy, and industry leadership. Their legacy was significant, but their digital presence had become fragmented and outdated—failing to reflect the modern, dynamic community they represent.

The previous website was difficult to navigate, lacked a cohesive information architecture, and didn't adequately serve the diverse needs of students, early-career professionals, experienced engineers, and corporate members. They needed a platform that would modernize their image while unifying their community around shared values of innovation, excellence, and social responsibility.

Our Approach

We conducted extensive research across the membership, interviewing students, practicing engineers, academics, and industry partners to understand their needs and pain points. The insights revealed a clear need for a hub-like experience that would serve as the go-to destination for career guidance, professional development, networking, and industry thought leadership.

  • Stakeholder interviews across 15+ organizations and hundreds of members
  • Competitive benchmark against leading professional bodies and engineering organizations globally
  • User journey mapping for distinct audience segments
  • Content audit and taxonomy development for 300+ resources and documents
  • Accessibility-first design ensuring WCAG AAA compliance

The strategic insight was positioning Engineers Ireland not just as a membership organization, but as the definitive digital hub for Ireland's entire engineering community—a trusted resource for career development, knowledge sharing, and professional advancement.

Digital Strategy

The new website consolidates membership management, professional development resources, career guidance, and industry advocacy into a unified platform. Key features include an intelligent member portal, comprehensive learning hub with curated courses and certifications, a job board connecting employers with vetted candidates, and a thought leadership section positioning Engineers Ireland as industry authorities.

The information architecture prioritizes discoverability—a prospective member can explore the value of membership while a current member can access CPD resources, update credentials, and connect with peers. Mobile-first design ensures accessibility for the growing segment of younger engineers who interact primarily through mobile devices.

Experience Design

Every interaction has been designed with empathy for the user's goal. Membership renewal is streamlined to under two minutes. Career guidance is personalized based on experience level and discipline. Professional development content is filterable by specialization, time commitment, and relevance to career progression. The community hub enables peer-to-peer knowledge sharing through discussion forums, event networking, and collaborative working groups.

This new platform finally reflects who we are as a community. It's modern, it's intuitive, and it genuinely serves the needs of every engineer in Ireland from students to seasoned professionals. The feedback from our members has been overwhelmingly positive.
— Conor O'Connell, CEO, Engineers Ireland

The Results

Within the first three months of launch, the new platform drove a 180% increase in member engagement, 92% reduction in support inquiries thanks to improved self-service, and 45% increase in professional development course registrations. Member retention improved by 12%, and the new job board generated 500+ employer partnerships in the first quarter. The platform has become the essential resource for Ireland's engineering community.
